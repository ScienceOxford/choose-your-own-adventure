Share and View Projects

These instructions show you how to share your story.


  • Save and share your project on Slack
  • View the projects shared on Slack

If you read a story that someone else shared, remember to comment on their post to let them know which part you liked best, and if you noticed any bugs that they could fix!

Do not share other people's projects without their permission.


Sharing your Story

Save your story to your computer, by clicking the menu next to the story title on the bottom left, then clicking 'Publish to File'.


This will save it on your computer - possibly in your downloads folder.

In Slack, go to the #showcase channel and begin a new post to let us know that you are sharing your story.

On the right-hand side of your post, click the paperclip icon to attach your story. Click 'Your computer' to choose your file.


Find the story you just downloaded, add it to your post, then send it.

When you have finished, your post will look like the picture below.



Viewing other Stories

In Slack, the uploaded stories are code - you cannot read the story the way the author means you to.

To view a story properly, you first need to download it. Hover over the post, then click the download icon.


This will save their story to your computer, possibly in your downloads folder. Find the new file and open it, you should now be able to read it.

Test it out, trying out a few different branches, and leave them a comment to let them know that you have read it!

  • What do you think of their story?
  • Have they added any cool features?
  • Did anything unexpected happen?
